In Task 5, Jaclyn attempted to use t.test() to calculate confidence intervals (CIs). Although her implementation had errors, it inspired me to explore t.test() as an alternative to my method of manually calculating CIs using qnorm() for normal variables (ci \<- mean + qnorm(c(0.025, 0.975)) \* se). After testing, I realized it can be a better option for small samples (n=30) since it uses the t-distribution, for additional uncertainty compared to the normal distribution I used. I’ll consider adopting this for my own code, especially for normally distributed variables.
